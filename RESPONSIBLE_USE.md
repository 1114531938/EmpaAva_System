# Responsible Use Policy

EmpaAva is released for research and evaluation of embodied empathetic agents.
Use the system transparently, obtain informed consent for captured audio/video,
and make clear that users are interacting with an AI-generated avatar.

The project must not be used to:

- impersonate a real person or falsely imply their participation, endorsement,
  identity, or statements;
- clone, synthesize, publish, or redistribute a person's face, likeness, or voice
  without authorization;
- deceive, defraud, harass, threaten, exploit, discriminate against, or manipulate
  a person or protected group;
- collect, retain, disclose, or train on personal audio/video without a lawful
  basis, informed consent, and appropriate security controls;
- generate unlawful content or infringe privacy, publicity, copyright, trademark,
  confidentiality, or other third-party rights;
- present EmpaAva as a doctor, therapist, crisis service, or substitute for
  professional medical or mental-health care;
- deploy the system in high-impact decisions or safety-critical settings without
  qualified human oversight, risk assessment, and all required approvals.

Operators are responsible for access controls, retention limits, deletion and
export mechanisms, incident response, disclosure of synthetic media, and
compliance with applicable law and every third-party license listed in
[`THIRD_PARTY_LICENSES.md`](THIRD_PARTY_LICENSES.md).

If a conversation indicates imminent self-harm, violence, abuse, or another
emergency, the system should avoid diagnostic or prescriptive claims and direct
the user to trusted people, qualified professionals, or local emergency services.
